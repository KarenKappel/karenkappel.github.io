About me

As a user experience designer, I focus on the interaction between products and users. When designing I go through a process to ensure the best outcome. I'm always interested and open to learning new tools, at the moment I am able work with Adobe Experience Design, Sketch, Adobe Illustrator and Principle.

Methods and research methods I use

Interviews

Interviewing users, to get a general idea of their experience with a service or product as well as understanding their general pain points.

Emotional journey

Visualizing the users journey with the service or product and displaying the point where emotions are at their peak, negative and positive.

Customer journey

Visualizing the users experience and their interaction with the product or service.

Tasks list

Writing down the main task and the sub tasks the user has to follow in oder to accomplish their goal. In order to create a better flow when users interact with a product or service.

Card sorting

In order to find out which flow or structure users follow. With this knowlegde I can then later create a better work flow or organise information according to their mental model.

Questionairre

In order to verify previous user research or to start user research with limitations so the the results can be used during the design process.

User stories

User stories help focus on what the end users would like to achieve, this also helps with finding out wich features should be on the user requirement list.

User requirement list

When sketching I like to keep the user requirement list and flowchart as a refenerence for wich features and elements should go where.

MoSCoW

As a designer I like to focus on features that are most wanted/needed first, with this list I can easily see which features are required first.

Persona

I like to user personas to summarize the user research and make the results comprehensive to all stakeholders involved.

Sketching/Wireframes

Sketching ideas, wireframes and hi-fi sketches of the pages and flow of the product in order to try out new ideas and test new solutions before I start working on a mockup on the computer.

Prototyping

Creating a mockup that can be tested on the computer, with Adobe XD, Sketch, or Principle.

Usability testing

Testing with users, and non-users, is one of the most important steps during the entire process. This will give insights about the solutions and how the users think and feel anout the design.
