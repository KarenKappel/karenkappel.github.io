About me

As a user experience designer, I focus on the interaction between products and users. Design thinking and curiousity form the basis of my work flow. When possible I go through the different steps of the design process, favouring the exploratory, conceptualising phases and roadmapping towards future oppertunities within the project. In order to make this possible I use a set of tools: Figma(or Sketch, AdobeXD), Miro, Jira, Trello, ChatGPT. I use these for flowcharting, sketching, wireframing, designing, brainstorming and documentation for stories or projects I am working on.

Methods and research methods I use

Flowcharting

In order to understand the flow of the feature, application or tool I usually start with visualising the flows in a flowchart. This also to have a starting point with research and/or sketching in addition to having a easy visualation to share with product owners, product managers, and programmers.

Sketching/Wireframes

Sketching is something I do throughout the entire proces, depending on the topic at thand this could range from low-fi to high-fi sketches and eventually if needed wireframes. I favour communicating ideas and flows in mid-fi sketches, because they focus on the feature with enough detail that users and stakeholders can focus on tech or ux issues.

Tasks list

Writing down and understainding the high and low level goals forms the basis of the task list. I prefer working with a task list in combination with MoSCoW so the entire team can focus on creating a minimal viable product but don't lose sight of the wishes and needs of the users.

User stories

User stories help focus on what the end users would like to achieve, this also helps with finding out wich features should be on the user requirement list.

User requirement list

When sketching I like to keep the user requirement list and flowchart as a refenerence for wich features and elements should go where.

MoSCoW

As a designer I like to focus on features that are most wanted/needed first, with this list I can easily see which features are required first.

Questionairre

In order to verify previous user research or to start user research with limitations so the the results can be used during the design process.

Interviews

Interviewing users, to get a general idea of their experience with a service or product as well as understanding their general pain points.

Card sorting

In order to find out which flow or structure users follow. With this knowlegde I can then later create a better work flow or organise information according to their mental model.

Emotional journey

Visualizing the users journey with the service or product and displaying the point where emotions are at their peak, negative and positive.

Customer journey

Visualizing the users experience and their interaction with the product or service.

Persona

I like to user personas to summarize the user research and make the results comprehensive to all stakeholders involved.

Prototyping

Creating a mockup that can be tested on the computer, with Adobe XD, Sketch, or Principle.

Usability testing

Testing with users, and non-users, is one of the most important steps during the entire process. This will give insights about the solutions and how the users think and feel anout the design.
