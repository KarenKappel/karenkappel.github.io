Projects / Buurt in zicht

Buurt in zicht

Concept

An online platform, Buurt in zicht, will act as an information source where potential buyers and tenants can see data that is decisive for choosing a particular neighborhood.

Design process

The main objective of this project was to visualise data in a graphically pleasing yet informational way. At first, I started by asking myself and others what kind of data they would wanted. After gathering their feedback, I concluded a lot of people were interested in the neighbourhoods they live, but had a hard time looking up information about it. This feedback later grew to a concept and with feedback from my project supervisor it grew to a platform were people can find and compare neighbourhoods.

The biggest challenges during this project were, finding a way to combine and visualise large amount of data while still being recognisable by the average user, using a colour pallet that had enough different colours and could also be used on screens and could be sign by people with visual impairments, and staying in line with the visual guide of the municipality of Rotterdam. I chose to design for this city because they had the most data available.

Usere research

To make sure I visualised data users wanted and needed I looked at online recourse about how people buy houses and what kind, if at all, of research they did. I also went to different home owners and tenants to ask them why they specifically chose the house they live in and if they had the option of moving what they would do different. According to this research usres were mostly interested in the following information.

Insights

  • Housing stock
  • Types of public transport
  • Scholing oppurtunies
  • Type of house
  • Valuation of real estate
  • Available parking spaces

Design

This is the main page where users can select one or more neighbourhoods in Rotterdam. I chose to use an interactive map so users can easily see where these neighbourhoods are located and see the size differences between them. After selecting some neighbourhoods the users can see the differences between them in three overarching categories and 18 subcategories.
